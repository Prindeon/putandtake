Danish Golden Trout

Danish golden trout feature a vibrant golden-yellow to deep orange color, adorned with scattered black spots across their body. They're distinguished by a prominent red to orange stripe running along their sides.

Max. weight: 0.9 kg
Max. length: 30 cm

How to catch?

Utilize lightweight fishing tackle, including light spinning or fly rods with small reels and line, and experiment with baits or lures mimicking their natural prey like small spinners, flies, or worms. Present your bait naturally, avoiding startling the fish, and pay attention to ideal fishing times, typically during early morning or late afternoon when insect activity is high. Be patient, observant, and practice responsible catch and release to conserve these populations.

The diet of Danish golden trout primarily consists of a variety of aquatic insects, crustaceans, and small fish found within their freshwater habitats.

Rainbow Trout

The rainbow trout is named for the red stripe that runs along its side. It has a sleek body with a shiny silver color that turns into a light pink stripe down its side. The back is a darker olive-green with tiny black spots that go over its back fin and tail.

Max. weight: 12 kg
Max. length: 80 cm

How to catch?

Start by using light to medium tackle to feel the trout’s light bite. Bait such as worms, corn, or small dough baits are highly effective, especially when fished near the bottom or floated under a bobber. For lure fishing, small spinners or spoons that mimic small fish can attract trout.

The rainbow trout primarily feeds on amphipods, snails, leeches, flies, grasshoppers, beetles, small crustaceans, and insect larvae. Large rainbow trout also feed on small fish.
